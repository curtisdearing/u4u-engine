# Dossier Notes
Sasank's thought process and notes when updating the dossier sample.






First section:
 information from patient that is going into our algorithm
 Demographics, what genetic info we have (vcf, etc.), what the patient's individual health goals are (important)
 This cements the individual patient as the focus. It also informs ppl what info is being used in the subsequent analyses. Additionally, it serves as CYA if a pt has lame outcomes when they didn't have much info on file for us to use.
Our sample patient should be a 56 y/o woman, who wants to (1) lose weight, (2) reduce inflammation in her osteoarthritic knees, and reduce wrinkles in her skin.
 This is a much more normal target audience for boutique clinics, and assures investors we are not just gunning for some tiny market of 20-something men who want to inject "wolverine" peptide.
Second section:
 Safety.
 Any possible safety info, such as contraindications from medical history, family history, genetics, should be here.
 It is okay to have a bit more here as safety is important. (As is our demonstrated "commitment" to safety.)
Third section:
 Connect health goals to possible peptides
 This section should have one row for each health goal, with an arrow pointing from health goal on left to possible peptides on right
 I think we can go ahead and put dosing information (recommendations?) in this same row-block
 Upper bounds and starting doses need to come from FDA or clinical trials.
 Additional dosing information (why a person might need more or less) should come from "us" but easily traceable to hard sources (studies, etc).



Our priorities:
Patient centered care/personalized care. Both in genetics and focusing on the health goals of the patient
Safety of the patient
Effectiveness of the therapy

This dossier will be used in a couple ways.
First, it will be printed out by the clinician in clinic who will talk with the patient about it, using it as a reference.
Second, it will go home with the patient, who will be able to look at it later to understand why the proposed therapy is right for them.

So we need this to contain information about our highest priorities. We need it to be readable by patients. We need this to convince our customers (clinicians) and patients that the therapies are in the best interest of the patients.
